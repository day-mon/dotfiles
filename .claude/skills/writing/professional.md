# Professional writing style

For docs, PRs, emails, design/spec docs, anything external- or work-facing.

## Voice
- Clear, concise, and courteous. Respect the reader's time.
- Lead with the point, then support it.
- Contractions are fine in moderation; keep the tone human, not stiff.
- Active voice. Concrete nouns and verbs over abstractions.

## Formatting
- Proper capitalization and complete sentences.
- Structure with headings and bullets so it scans.
- Define acronyms and technical terms on first use.

## Punctuation
- **Never use em dashes (—).** Use a comma, a colon, parentheses, or rewrite the sentence.
- En dashes for ranges are fine (Q1–Q2).
- Prefer a period over a semicolon when a sentence is getting long.

## Avoid
- Hype and filler: world-class, cutting-edge, leverage, seamless, revolutionary, robust.
- Hedging ("just", "maybe", "I think") when you can state the thing directly.
- Walls of text. Break it up.
- Unsupported claims. Say what you know; flag what you don't.
